How does Invisalign work?

The treatment process begins with a consultation where Dr. Asrar examines the patient's mouth, reviews X-rays, and determines candidacy. If approved, the team provides a treatment timeline and cost estimate and uses an iTero scanner to capture a digital impression.

After instruction on usage and cleaning, patients wear aligners for between 20 and 22 hours each day. Progress checks occur every eight or so weeks, during which Dr. Asrar verifies proper tooth adjustment and provides the next series of aligners in your treatment plan until you achieve your final results.

Invisalign possesses effectiveness nearly equal in many cases to metal braces and can address conditions from slightly crooked teeth to more major dental problems.

Understanding the cost of Invisalign

Cost varies because there isn't a one-size-fits-all treatment plan. Price estimates are provided during consultations. During the consultation, our team determines:

Invisalign vs. mail-in aligners

Mail-in aligners typically cost less but cut corners to cut costs, like bypassing the initial exam and relying on DIY impressions. Some companies lack licensed dental professionals to oversee the teeth-straightening process, potentially resulting in worsened misalignment.

Frequently asked questions

Can you get Invisalign after braces?

Yes. Many patients who experienced orthodontic relapse in the years following their time with traditional braces can achieve a straight smile again with Invisalign.

Can you eat with Invisalign trays in?

Eating with aligners on can cause them to stain, warp, and even break. Patients should remove aligners, rinse with clean, cool water, and place them in their case to prevent loss or damage.

What should I do if my Invisalign tray broke?

Small hairline cracks allow continued wear while exercising extreme caution to prevent further damage. Substantial breaks require office contact to determine whether proceeding to the next aligner or reverting to the previous set is appropriate.

Important: You should NOT use superglue or any other household adhesives to attempt to repair a broken aligner.

How do I clean my Invisalign?

Rinse with cool water when removed, then use clear, mild dishwashing liquid and a damp, soft-bristled toothbrush for gentle scrubbing. Cleaning crystals from the welcome kit may be used for deeper cleaning or extended wear.

Will I need to wear a retainer after Invisalign treatment?

Yes. Consistently wearing a retainer plays a crucial role in preventing orthodontic relapse — when teeth drift back into their previously misaligned positions following orthodontic treatment.

How long does Invisalign take?

While traditional braces typically require 2–3 years, Invisalign's average treatment timeline is only 12–18 months. Minor cases may be completed in 4–8 months.

How treatment works

Traditional braces function through a bracket-and-wire system. The orthodontist attaches small metal brackets to each tooth's center, then threads wire across them. As tension adjusts over time, teeth gradually shift into proper alignment. Elastics — small rubber bands — can address various bite issues including open bite, crossbite, overbite, and underbite.

Who is a good candidate?

Ideal candidates have:

  • Healthy, strong, stable teeth capable of withstanding archwire pressure
  • Bite misalignment without underlying jaw issues
  • Crooked, overlapping, or gapped teeth requiring correction
  • Commitment to excellent oral hygiene throughout treatment

Treatment benefits

  • Reduced risk of gum disease and cavities
  • Improved digestion and fewer gastrointestinal problems
  • Decreased jaw stress and teeth grinding
  • Prevention of jawbone erosion
  • Dramatically lower temporomandibular joint (TMJ) disorder risk
  • Enhanced confidence and self-esteem

Self-ligating option

Unlike traditional fixed brackets, self-ligating braces feature a gate-like sliding mechanism allowing the archwire to move as teeth shift. Adjustment visits are typically shorter and less uncomfortable.

Treatment duration

Most patients require 12 to 24 months of active treatment, followed by consistent retainer use. More complex cases may take longer, especially as patients age.

Who is a good candidate for clear/ceramic braces?

The best candidates for clear or ceramic braces in Round Rock are those who:

  • Want a discreet appearance. Clear/ceramic braces are much more discreet than shiny metal brackets and wires, which is why they're a great option for younger or older patients who are image conscious.
  • Have moderate to more extensive orthodontic issues. Similar to traditional braces, ceramic braces can address a wide range of orthodontic problems, including bite misalignment, crowding, gaps, and more.
  • Are dedicated to good oral hygiene. It's important to maintain good at-home oral hygiene habits to stay on track with your treatment timeline.
  • Want to straighten their smile faster. Dr. Asrar uses 3M Advanced Clarity ceramic brackets that take less time to place, are low-profile, and have been shown to straighten teeth in less time.

How do ceramic braces work?

Ceramic braces work identically to traditional metal braces. Instead of placing bulky metal brackets, Dr. Asrar will secure 3M Clarity Advanced Ceramic Brackets to the front surface of teeth. These are much smaller, have a lower profile, and can make treatment much more comfortable for patients, all while offering similarly effective results as traditional braces. They also tend to be much faster and more efficient at bonding to the teeth, making the first appointment you have with us more streamlined and comfortable.

After the ceramic brackets are adhered to the teeth, Dr. Asrar and our team at Big Smile Orthodontics will secure a tooth-colored archwire through them to place pressure onto the teeth that will allow them to move. Every six to eight weeks, you'll return to our office for routine check-in visits and to make any necessary adjustments.

Benefits over traditional metal braces

The biggest benefit that ceramic braces have over traditional metal braces, and the top reason why patients choose them, is because they're much more discreet. They offer a lower profile and clear appearance that blends in with the natural color of the teeth that they're adhered to. This makes them perfect for adults who are trying to maintain a more professional image as well as teens who are image conscious.

In addition to them being hardly noticeable, our 3M Clarity Advanced Ceramic Brackets also take less time to adhere and have a smooth surface and rounded corners. This can make your initial appointment with us much faster as well as your overall treatment experience more comfortable. Overall, these ceramic brackets have also been shown to offer slightly faster results due to their incredibly effective design.

Phase 1 early treatment

Ages 7–10 · Growth guidance

Phase 1 orthodontics serves children with mixed dentition — a combination of permanent and temporary teeth. This early intervention enables Dr. Asrar to guide children's smile development in ways that reduce the need for more extensive orthodontic treatments later, ultimately conserving resources and time.

Does my child need Phase 1 orthodontics?

Phase 1 orthodontics is typically recommended for young patients, usually between ages seven and 10. Three main indicators suggest your child could benefit:

  • Bite misalignment. If your child exhibits a crossbite, open bite, underbite, or overbite, screening by an orthodontist is advisable. Dr. Asrar can determine whether early intervention is necessary.
  • Thumb-sucking past age four. While thumb-sucking is natural for babies, continued suction after teeth emerge can alter permanent tooth alignment and bite, potentially causing crowding that Phase 1 treatment addresses.
  • Airway problems. Sleep-breathing disorders can result from orthodontic issues. Chronic snoring or daytime fatigue in children may be addressable before worsening.

Benefits of Phase 1 treatment

  • Correcting habits like tongue thrust or thumb sucking that could cause developmental and dental problems
  • Improved prognosis for permanent tooth eruption
  • Reduced injury risk for protruding teeth
  • Guiding jaw growth to accommodate emerging teeth
  • Avoiding more invasive dental corrections later
  • Increased confidence and self-esteem

What happens after Phase 1?

Following treatment completion, Dr. Asrar allows children an appliance-free period of approximately one year before Phase 2 begins. This enables transition from mixed dentition to primarily permanent teeth. Phase 2 focuses on permanent tooth alignment and is typically less extensive.

Retainers & appliances

Helping you get the most out of your hard-earned results

After completing orthodontic treatment, patients enter the maintenance phase requiring either fixed or removable retainers for a specified duration. For patients with narrow palates, a palatal expander may be necessary before treatment. Dr. Asrar discusses all available oral appliance and retainer options during initial consultations.

We also offer complimentary take-home whitening for Invisalign patients — maintain your results and brighten your smile at the same time.

Removable retainers

Dr. Asrar may recommend removable retainers post-treatment to prevent newly straightened teeth from shifting. Patients wear them as prescribed, potentially transitioning to nighttime-only wear later. Because teeth have a type of muscle memory, retainers act to help preserve your results.

Fixed retainers

Patients requiring extensive orthodontic intervention may receive fixed retainers. These are permanently secured inside the mouth on tooth interiors, remaining invisible. Made of stainless steel wire, they prevent teeth from reverting to original positions.

Palatal expanders

This treatment combines tooth movement and jaw expansion by widening the upper jaw's two halves — the palate — which are joined by a suture in the roof of the mouth. Expanders cause minimal discomfort beyond initial pressure and temporary speech changes.

Detailed emergency types & responses

Toothache

Is a toothache an emergency? Some level of dental pain is to be expected during any type of orthodontic treatment. The good news is that it's usually completely normal to experience mouth-wide aching and soreness when biting down. These symptoms may last for a few days after first getting your braces or aligners, as well as after having them tightened or advancing to a new set.

However, in some cases you may be experiencing a dental emergency if the pain does not go away, seems to be getting worse, your tooth is becoming discolored, you have a sharp pain when biting down, or are experiencing lingering sensitivity.

How to handle it: If your tooth pain does not go away within a few days, please call us as soon as possible so we can bring you in and determine what's causing the problem. We may also instruct you to call your regular dentist for an emergency appointment if necessary. In the meantime, you can take over-the-counter pain relievers, apply icepacks, and stick to cold foods and beverages to ease any swelling or discomfort. You can also rinse your mouth with saltwater to kill any bacteria from a potential infection and use dental floss to remove stuck debris.

Loose bracket or wire

Is it an emergency? Because this type of emergency typically doesn't cause pain, it doesn't usually require urgent care. However, you should still have it fixed soon to ensure your treatment stays on track, so be sure to call us as soon as possible to set up an appointment.

How to handle it: If your bracket or wire is completely off, do your best to locate it and bring it with you to your appointment. If it is still somewhat in place, try holding it on with orthodontic wax. In most cases we should be able to simply reattach it and restabilize the rest of your orthodontics right away.

Broken bracket or wire

Is it an emergency? This type of incident could require more urgent care than a loose wire or bracket, because there is a risk it could injure your soft tissues. Never attempt to fix this type of problem yourself, as it could not only damage your braces, but it could harm your teeth and gums. Instead, call us right away for assistance.

How to handle it: Locate any pieces that may have broken off and be sure to bring them with you to your appointment. In the meantime, you can cover any sharp pieces in your mouth with dental wax or sugarless gum.

Protruding/poking wire

Is it an emergency? While it may not be an immediate problem, over time the irritation caused by a poking wire could lead to an infection in the soft tissues of your mouth. Thus, you should treat it as an urgent matter and contact us as soon as possible.

How to handle it: Please call us to schedule an urgent appointment. In the meantime, you can use a cotton swab or clean pencil eraser to gently push the protruding wire back into place or apply some dental wax or sugarless gum over the wire to prevent it from injuring your cheeks, lips, or tongue. We can help you put it back into place and make sure your orthodontics are working as intended when you arrive.

Mouth sores or irritation

Is it an emergency? Much like toothaches, mouth sores from braces are completely normal, as your soft tissues need time to adjust to the presence of your new brackets and wires. These should heal and become more comfortable within a few weeks.

How to handle it: If your mouth sores don't seem to be getting any better, or if they begin hurting more, please call us as soon as possible. We can inspect the area for infection and help you determine how best to ease the pain.

Broken ligature

Is it an emergency? A broken ligature is not usually considered an emergency, but it should be fixed as soon as possible to prevent it from poking your soft tissues. In the meantime, cover it with dental wax or sugarless gum, and call us right away to schedule an appointment.

How to handle it: Depending on the type of ligature, you may be able to cover it with dental wax until it can be repaired. Please call us as soon as possible and we can give you more specific instructions for your individual situation, as well as schedule a time for you to come in and have it fixed.

Cut lips, cheeks, or tongue

Is it an emergency? Should you accidentally sustain a blow to the face, your braces could cut your soft tissues. While this type of injury may bleed a lot, it is not typically an emergency, but we can stitch the wound closed if necessary. However, if the bleeding hasn't stopped on its own within 10 to 15 minutes, please go to your nearest emergency room for help.

How to handle it: First, gently clean the area with a wet washcloth or by rinsing your mouth out with water. Then, apply pressure to the area to stop the bleeding. You can also use over-the-counter pain reliever and cold compresses to ease any discomfort.

Something stuck between teeth

Is it an emergency? Getting food trapped between the teeth or between the teeth and your braces is pretty common during orthodontic treatment. It is important, however, to remove it proactively instead of just hoping it will loosen and dislodge itself. Leaving it in place can not only be painful, but it can also cause other problems like cavities and gum disease.

How to handle it: First, try dislodging the debris by rinsing your mouth with warm water. Next try using dental floss to remove it, or a water irrigator if you have one. Be sure to stock up on things like floss threaders, picks, and interdental brushes to have on hand for this type of situation and never use a sharp tool like a toothpick. If you're still unable to dislodge it, please call us for help.

Broken/lost aligner or retainer

Is it an emergency? Although a lost or broken aligner isn't usually considered an emergency, delaying getting help for it can potentially cause delays in your treatment or set it off track. Thus, be sure to contact us as soon as possible for help.

How to handle it: Never wear a broken aligner or retainer, as they could potentially move your teeth into the wrong positions. If you've lost an aligner and can't find it by retracing your steps, give us a call and we can advise you. In some cases, you may be able to advance to the next set of trays, or it is possible that you may need to have the missing one replaced.

Loose teeth

Is it an emergency? The feeling of loose teeth can be especially alarming if you've got all your permanent teeth, but it's very normal during orthodontic treatment. After all, your teeth are changing position, so they need to loosen a bit to move. However, should they feel particularly loose, something could be wrong, especially if you have signs of gum disease like bleeding or inflammation. Thus, if you're concerned about a loose tooth, please contact us right away.

How to handle it: Never play with or overuse loose teeth, as this could make them worse. Be sure to practice excellent oral hygiene to protect your teeth from bacteria that could weaken the tissue in your mouth. If you are worried, we can bring you in for X-rays to keep an eye on the health of your jawbone and determine if you need gum disease treatment or a bone graft.

Emergency FAQs

Will a toothache go away on its own?

It is normal to feel soreness from time to time throughout your orthodontic treatment, especially in the hours immediately following your adjustment appointments. The good news is that it's temporary. Plus, there are a few steps you can take to alleviate your discomfort even sooner, including using a cold compress, rinsing with warm saltwater, and taking OTC pain medication as directed.

How should I sleep with tooth pain?

If you're experiencing discomfort stemming from the movement of your teeth, there are a few steps you can take to alleviate it long enough to get a good night's sleep. The first step is opting for extremely soft foods at dinner, like cottage cheese, mashed potatoes, and cooked carrots. Then, about 30 minutes before bed, take OTC pain medication (never more than the recommended amount) and use a cold compress for 10 minutes at a time.

Tip: If there are any sharp edges of your brackets or wires, place some dental wax over them so they don't irritate the soft tissue in your mouth.

Is it safe to use a toothpick?

If you get a piece of food stuck between your brackets and wires, don't use a toothpick. The sharp edge can push the debris further into place and irritate your gums further. Plus, accidentally swallowing a toothpick can be fatal. So, opt for pre-threaded floss instead. If that doesn't work, give us a call.

What does chronic bad breath mean?

If you're struggling with a persistent odor on your breath, take a good look at your oral hygiene regimen. In addition to brushing and flossing consistently, you should clean your brackets and wires, gently scrub the surface of your tongue, and rinse with mouthwash consistently. Then, do your best to steer clear of pungent foods, like onions, pickles, and garlic.

If you've adjusted your oral hygiene regimen and your diet, but the unpleasant smell persists, then schedule an appointment at our office. That way, we can determine if the odor is stemming from an untreated oral health problem, like a large cavity.

What if I can't afford emergency dentistry?

While some patients come to us with an easy fix, like a wire that needs to be put back in place, others require more extensive treatment to get back on track. No matter what the case is for you, you can have peace of mind knowing that our emergency orthodontist in Round Rock will provide you with an estimate of the cost upfront. Dr. Asrar will also discuss the different financial solutions that are available, including flexible financing with CareCredit. That way, you can focus on getting the care you need instead of stressing about the price.

Life with braces

Seamlessly navigate your life with braces

Eating with braces

During your orthodontic treatment, our team recommends avoiding hard, sticky, and tough food items like toffees, ice, or jolly ranchers. It is always advisable to start with soft foods to go through an adjustment phase and then progressively incorporate regular foods on a daily basis.

Loosening of teeth

Part of the process of creating your new, beautiful smile is moving your teeth; to move them, they need to loosen up first. "Loose teeth" are a natural part of the process. Once your teeth are in the right place, they will settle into their new position. Call our office in Round Rock for orthodontic treatment information.

General soreness

Braces do not hurt; however, soreness can be expected. You will experience this for a few days when your braces are first placed and then for a day or so after each adjustment. We recommend you use an over-the-counter pain reliever and a salt water rinse to help deal with the discomfort. If the appliance is causing irritation, cover that part with dental wax. Do not hesitate to contact us with any questions or concerns.

Loose wire, bracket, or band

Besides some soreness, it is normal to expect some loosening of teeth. That is essentially how teeth move, so do not be alarmed. Wires and brackets can also come loose. When they do, give us a call. If you have a loose wire, you can try to push it back into place with the eraser end of a pencil until you can see us. If a part comes off, save it and bring it to the appointment. Please use wax as needed to help with any discomfort, and we will be happy to see you to resolve the issue.

Care of appliance & brushing

The goal of our orthodontic team in Round Rock is to help you achieve a beautiful straight smile with a functional bite, and maintain the overall health of your teeth and gums. To that effect, it's very important to brush and floss diligently during your treatment and continue your cleaning appointments with your general dentist. An electric toothbrush helps remove plaque effectively, and diligent flossing or use of a Waterpik will help prevent cavities in between your teeth. Let us know if a demonstration of proper technique is required.
